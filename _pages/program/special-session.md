## Tech for Tribes: Cultural Preservation, Social Equity and Enabling Access

<img src="/assets/images/committee/special_sessions/Vibhu-Nayar.jpeg" style="height: 225px; display: block; margin: 0 auto;">

### Shri. Vibhu Nayar, Senior Indian Administrative Service (IAS) Officer

**Date and Time:** 21 December 2025 (Sunday), 14:00 - 14:45hrs

**Abstract**

The Ministry of Tribal Affairs (MoTA) is the nodal ministry of the Government of India dedicated to the socio-economic development, education, welfare, and empowerment of Scheduled Tribes across the country. A significant part of its mandate involves preserving and promoting India’s diverse tribal cultures, including safeguarding and documenting tribal languages, many of which are endangered or have limited written resources.

In recent years, MoTA has supported initiatives related to digital inclusion, linguistic preservation, and technology-enabled learning, which naturally intersect with Natural Language Processing (NLP). Through collaborations with academic institutions and government missions, MoTA has encouraged efforts such as digitization of tribal language content, creation of language resources, development of educational tools in tribal languages, and exploration of NLP-based technologies to enhance accessibility, documentation, and cultural preservation. These efforts contribute to making tribal languages more visible in India’s broader digital and linguistic ecosystem.

**Speaker's Bio**

Vibhu Nayar is a senior Indian Administrative Service (IAS) officer of the 1990 batch (Tamil Nadu cadre) and served as Secretary, Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India. An alumnus of IIT Delhi and Govind Ballabh Pant University, he has held several leadership positions, including Executive Director of the India Trade Promotion Organisation (ITPO) and Chairman & Managing Director of MMTC Limited. Known for his strategic vision and commitment to inclusive development, he brings over three decades of experience spanning governance, trade, and public sector transformation. Till recently Secretary, Govt of India. He has been elected to the UN Secretary General’s Advisory Board and has conceptualised and led Field Level Leadership globally along with the World Bank .
Over the past years, he has worked on directing AI for disadvantaged communities and geographies


## BharatGen: Building India’s Sovereign, Multilingual AI

<img src="/assets/images/committee/special_sessions/Ganesh.png" style="height: 225px; display: block; margin: 0 auto;">

### Prof. Ganesh Ramakrishnan, IIT Bombay (Dept. of Computer Science and Engineering) & BharatGen (Lead PI)

**Date and Time:** 21 December 2025 (Sunday), 14:45 - 15:30hrs

**Abstract**

BharatGen is India's sovereign AI initiative dedicated to building foundational, multilingual, and multimodal intelligence that addresses the nation's diverse linguistic and societal needs. Supported by the Government of India and anchored at IIT Bombay, BharatGen develops large language models in speech, text, vision across 22 Indian languages. The initiative aims to democratize access to high-quality AI for sectors such as governance, agriculture, healthcare, education, legal services, finance, and more, while ensuring data sovereignty, cultural alignment, and technological independence. By uniting researchers, industry partners, and public institutions, BharatGen is empowering the country to shape its own AI future through open, inclusive, and scalable innovation.

**Speaker's Bio**

Prof. Ganesh Ramakrishnan is the Principal Investigator at BharatGen, India's landmark initiative to build sovereign, multilingual, and multimodal AI for 22 Indian languages. A distinguished academic and innovator, he brings over two decades of experience at the intersection of core research, applied AI, and systems deployment, making him one of the country's leading voices in inclusive digital transformation. He also serves as the Bank of Baroda Chair Professor in Digital Entrepreneurship at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, IIT Bombay. Prof. Ramakrishnan's work spans large language models, generative AI, human-assisted AI/ML, AI in resource-constrained environments, and the integration of symbolic domain knowledge into machine learning and natural language processing. His research has been featured in premier international conferences such as AAAI, ACL, NeurIPS, and EMNLP, where he has also contributed as an area chair. His contributions to the field have earned him recognition by Forbes India as one of the "Top 30 Indian Minds in AI" and by Analytics India Magazine as one of the "Top 100 Most Influential Global AI Leaders".



## CFILT Day (Special Session on CFILT)

<img src="/assets/images/committee/special_sessions/pb.jpg" style="height: 225px; display: block; margin: 0 auto;">

### In memory of Prof. Pushpak Bhattacharyya: Alumni of CFILT and Invited NLP researchers from India

**Date and Time:** 21 December 2025 (Sunday), 16:00 - 17:30hrs

**Abstract**

As part of IJCNLP–AACL 2025, we are hosting a special session titled "CFILT Day" dedicated to the Computation for Indian Language Technology (CFILT) to celebrate its remarkable journey of 25 years. This session will bring together CFILT alumni, faculty, students, and collaborators who have contributed to the lab’s growth in language technology research. As a highlight of the event, we will also be releasing the CFILT Silver Jubilee Booklet, showcasing the lab’s milestones, major research contributions, community initiatives, and the collective achievements of its alumni network. The CFILT Day celebration aims to strengthen connections across generations of researchers and commemorate the lab’s enduring impact on NLP in India. CFILT Day also stands as a heartfelt tribute to Prof. Pushpak Bhattacharyya, the visionary founder of CFILT, whose leadership, scholarship, and mentorship laid the foundation for the lab’s global impact. While Prof. Bhattacharyya is no longer with us, his intellectual legacy lives on through CFILT, its people, its research culture, and its continuing commitment to advancing Indian language technologies for society.  



## Special Session on Mission Bhashini

<img src="/assets/images/committee/special_sessions/Amitabh-Nag.jpeg" style="height: 225px; display: block; margin: 0 auto;">

### Shri. Amitabh Nag, CEO of Digital India's BHASHINI Division and Director at Digital India Corporation

**Date and Time:** 20 December 2025 (Saturday), 16:00 – 17:30hrs

**Abstract**

Mission Bhashini is India’s pioneering initiative to break digital and language barriers by making government services and digital platforms accessible in every major Indian language. Powered by cutting-edge AI and a “Voice-First” approach, Bhashini enables citizens especially those in rural and underserved communities to interact with technology effortlessly in their own language. With collaborations across ministries, startups, and industry, the mission is driving linguistic inclusivity in sectors like education, agriculture, healthcare, and governance, empowering millions to participate fully in India’s digital journey.

**Speaker's Bio**
Amitabh Nag, CEO of Digital India's BHASHINI Division and Director at Digital India Corporation, is responsible for leading the National Language Translation Mission (NLTM), a key initiative to eliminate language barriers in India. Additionally, he serves as the Director of India Dataset Platform (AI Kosh) and applications development initiative under the IndiaAI Mission. With over 30 years of experience, he is a seasoned business leader specializing in Business Management, Sales & Marketing, and IT Project Execution. He has successfully led business units driving IT transformation programs. Throughout his career, Amitabh Nag has held significant roles at major companies like Coforge, HP Inc., and TCS. He is known for his ability to manage large-scale projects, collaborate with cross-functional teams, and engage with external stakeholders to drive successful outcomes. He has been featured in Forbes India & Accel's list of "Top 30 Indian Minds in AI" and recognized as one of the Most Influential Global Leaders in AI by AIM 100, “100 Most Influential People in Tech and Innovation” in India by MIT Sloan Management Review India. He has received numerous accolades, including the Digital Trailblazer Award by Express Computer for his contributions to India's AI ecosystem. 
He was also named Impact Leader of the Year at the 2024 Global Spin Innovation Summit and honoured with the Leadership Award for Digital Inclusion by iLouge Media."
